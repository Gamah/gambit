## The goal, one sentence

A seated terry **reaches out and moves the piece with its hand**, like a person playing chess.
M13 shipped the seated bodies (gated behind `ChessRing.TerrySeated`); this adds the working
(right) arm. Chess is one-handed ‚Äî right hand only, always.

## The design, decided by the owner ‚Äî DO NOT relitigate

1. **The hand moves the piece.** We are making a chess game about people playing chess; the
   people move the pieces. The hand is the agent, the piece is what it moves. This is the north
   star ‚Äî every ambiguity resolves toward "what would a hand do."
2. **Move-only hand.** The hand animates ONLY for a committed move. While a player is *deciding*
   (hovering, selecting) the hand does **not** track the cursor and does **not** reach ‚Äî it sits
   in its rest pose. (This alone deletes the entire "thinking too snappy" problem and the whole
   hover-vs-move tempo question.)
3. **Anchored rest.** Between moves the working arm holds a fixed, composed rest pose (forearm/
   hand on the table edge) via a light IK anchor ‚Äî a consistent spot every move starts and ends
   at. Not released to the raw sit animation; not tracking anything.
4. **Flat slide, hand follows the piece.** For v1 the piece stays on the board surface and
   slides (no lift-off-the-board arc). The hand rides just above it. Keep it simple; a lift can
   come later.
5. **Keep the half-rise, but MOVE-ONLY.** For a piece past the seated arm's reach, the terry may
   rise/lean off the chair to reach it ‚Äî but ONLY while executing a move, **never on hover**.
   Attempt 2's fatal tic was the body heaving up on every *hovered* far square. **Get near moves
   working first (Phase 1); add the rise for far reach after (Phase 2).**
6. **Far, still-unreachable ‚Üí the hand trails.** If even the risen arm can't reach the
   destination, the hand follows the piece as far as it goes and the piece completes its slide
   alone. A hand reaching *after* a piece reads fine; only the failed *grab* ever read as broken.
7. **Minimal captures.** The attacker moves as any move (hand follows it to the square). The
   taken piece slides to its owner's tray **on its own, no hand, no exchange beat.**
8. **Fixed pinch grip.** One canned pinch pose (the `holdtype` blend) while the hand is on a
   piece. **No per-finger work in v1** ‚Äî there is no finger IK anyway (see facts), and the
   fingers were never what read as broken.
9. **Everyone sees the hand, including the mover.** No per-viewer special-casing; your own terry
   animates your move the same as anyone else's.
10. **Move tempo is ONE tunable slider, ~0.8‚Äì1s rest-to-rest**, deliberate not hurried. And ‚Äî
    **every tuning slider must apply to ALL terries in the world at once**, not just the local
    one (see facts: process statics + a scene pusher makes this automatic; keep that pattern).

## The model, precisely ‚Äî one clock owns a move

This is the reconciliation of "the hand moves the piece" (owner's mental model) with "parent the
IK target to the piece" (the robustness trick that finally makes it not-broken). They are the
same thing done right:

- **The PIECE's trajectory is the authoritative path.** A slide from origin‚Üídestination across
  the board surface, authored by `ChessBoardView`. It is smooth and lands *exactly* on the
  square ‚Äî trivially correct, unlike anything the arm does.
- **The HAND's IK target is DERIVED from the piece every frame**: `hand_right` target =
  `piece.WorldPosition + gripOffset`. The hand therefore *cannot diverge* from the piece; the
  flaky thing (the arm) can only ever be a little off the *right* answer, never authoritative.
  **Attempt 2 did this backwards ‚Äî the piece rode the hand bone ‚Äî and inherited every arm flaw
  plus a grab handshake that never fired.**
- **The move plays on ONE timeline** (the ~1s budget), in three phases:
  1. **Approach** (~0.3s): the piece is FROZEN on its origin. The hand IK target is the origin
     piece; the arm travels there from the rest anchor. The hand *initiates*.
  2. **Carry** (~0.5s): the piece slides origin‚Üídestination (flat). The hand IK target tracks
     it. They move together ‚Äî this is "the hand moves the piece."
  3. **Release** (~0.2s): piece is on the destination; the grip opens and the IK target eases
     back to the rest anchor.
- **The piece does not start sliding until the Approach phase ENDS** ‚Äî a timeline gate, not a
  proximity test. This is the whole fix for "the piece moves before/without the hand." No grab
  radius, no `HoldForHand` distance check ‚Äî both were attempts to detect "the hand arrived" by
  geometry, and both failed. The clock knows when the hand has arrived because the clock *sent*
  it there.

## Engine facts we PAID for ‚Äî verified, keep

Verified against `sbox-public` and/or measured live in the editor during attempts 1‚Äì2. These are
the expensive ones; re-verify before trusting, but do not rediscover from zero.

1. **Only FOUR game-facing IK chains exist:** `hand_left`, `hand_right`, `foot_left`,
   `foot_right`. Each is a two-bone limb solve terminating at the wrist/ankle, driven by
   `SkinnedModelRenderer.SetIk(name, worldTransform)` (which sets `ik.{name}.enabled/position/
   rotation`). **There is NO finger/thumb/index IK anywhere** ‚Äî grep returns zero. Fingers are
   *posed*, never *solved*. So "drive the fingertip and let the joints follow" is not available.
2. **The hand IK consumes the target ROTATION, and that rotation drives the ELBOW pole**
   (`CitizenAnimationHelper` feeds `SetIk` a full world transform). Measured consequences:
   - **Roll the hand target to swing the elbow OUT of the torso** ‚Äî this is the fix for the
     "t-rex arm". Roll 0 traps the whole arm in a vertical plane and the elbow just drops.
   - **Derive the hand PITCH from the forearm's actual bearing, not a fixed value.** A fixed
     nose-down pitch hyper-flexes the wrist on a far/flat reach (the "super kinked wrist").
3. **Override TRANSLATIONS carry the whole bone subtree exactly; override ROTATIONS do NOT carry
   child bones** (measured: ~15.8u of shoulder travel budgeted from a rotation, ~3u
   materialised). So any per-bone finger posing needs an override *per phalanx* (this is exactly
   what the engine's own `VrHand` does).
4. **The animgraph solves IK BEFORE bone overrides apply.** If you move a bone the arm rides
   (pelvis/spine, for the half-rise), pre-compensate the IK target by that override's
   translation. A residual **~5u native warp** remains after everything (procedural/twist bones,
   unreadable via any API) ‚Äî attempt 2 steered it out with a closed-loop **servo** (measure last
   frame's final-hand-vs-true-ask error, integrate, clamp, decay). Keep the servo idea if/when
   the half-rise returns in Phase 2.
5. **`SetIk` wants a WORLD transform** (it does `WorldTransform.ToLocal` internally). Handing it
   a local one silently parks the hand near the room floor.
6. **The Citizen `SkinnedModelRenderer` must live on a `Body` CHILD GameObject**, never the
   controller's own GO ‚Äî the animator writes to it every frame and welds the player to world
   origin otherwise. (M13 fact, still true.)
7. **Seat pose: `SeatSitBack` is `26` on master RIGHT NOW, and that is TOO FAR FORWARD.**
   *(Corrected after attempt 3 ‚Äî this fact previously claimed master was 36 and it is not.)* The
   value `26` is the abandoned attempt-1 reach leftover (scooted forward from `36` to drag rank 1
   under the reaching hand) that was **never reverted** when M14 reset to end-of-M13. At `26` the
   seated chest is buried ~a third into the tabletop ‚Äî invisible from this host and from the
   seated player's own first-person view, so it has sat wrong on master unnoticed. `36` is the
   documented centre of the working band (forward of ~34 the belly enters the slab; back of ~38
   the elbows-on-table idle stops being reachable). **Attempt 4 should set it back to `36` as part
   of the seated-pose fix ‚Äî and, unlike the doc, actually change the code.**
8. **Tuning must be GLOBAL across every terry** (owner requirement). The pattern that delivers
   it: pose/tempo knobs are process **statics** (in the equivalent of `TerryPose`/
   `SeatedHandSpikes`); a **scene component pushes** them on change; every terry's driver reads
   the same statics, so one slider moves all terries at once. Each machine has its own statics; a
   joined client uses the host's saved scene values ‚Äî fine, tuning is an editor activity.
9. **The dev host cannot render or run engine code.** The reach geometry was proven ONLY because
   it was **Sandbox-free** (its own tiny vector type, no `Vector3`) and driven through a dotnet
   harness over all 64 squares. Put any real geometry in `Code/Chess/` with no engine
   dependency; it is the only thing that can be *proven* on this host. (Same doctrine as
   `BoardDiff`, `CapturedMaterial`, `MoveSpeech`.)

## The traps that killed attempts 1 and 2 ‚Äî each cost real time

- **The grab could never fire (attempt 2's core bug).** The piece-rides-hand carry grabbed on a
  3D distance ‚â§ `GrabRadius` (9u), but the hand hovers **10‚Äì14u above** the board (grasp/lift
  height) while the piece sits **on** it ‚Äî the distance was *always* > 9. So the piece never rode
  the hand; it waited out a 1.2s hold and slid on its own AFTER the arm finished ("the piece
  doesn't begin to move until the arm is done"). **The new model parents the hand to the piece
  and gates on the timeline exactly to make this class of bug impossible.**
- **The half-rise heaved on every hover.** The terry rose off the chair to reach each *hovered*
  square (eased fast), which read as "way too snappy thinking." **Hence: rise is move-only, hand
  rests during thinking.**
- **Tuning the wrong wire.** Two full rounds went into chase-rate sliders that were *confirmed
  live* (via a status dump) and changed nothing visible, because the motion was dominated by a
  different subsystem. **Build the live-values console dump on day one** so "is it even loaded /
  is this the right knob" is never a guess again.
- **A fixed-size seated citizen simply cannot reach the far half of the board with its arm**
  (~20u arm vs ~35u far corner ‚Äî attempt 1's whole finding). The half-rise was the answer; the
  flat-slide/hand-follows model sidesteps it for near moves and only needs it for far ones.
- **Slow ‚âÝ deliberate, and "painfully slow" was a misdiagnosis.** Attempt 2 shipped "slow on
  purpose" and it read as broken; then even a 0.73s gesture read as "painfully slow" because the
  piece was waiting out the 1.2s hold, not the gesture. **One shared clock for hand+piece, one
  tempo slider.**
- **The render body freezes at your approach heading (attempt 3's whole session).** See the
  attempt-3 callout at the top: `PlantOnSeat` turns the root, but the `Body` child GO's yaw was
  last written by `OnRotateRenderBody` and the controller is off by then, so it keeps the heading
  you were walking. Root faces the board; body doesn't. Latent on master. **Re-assert the render
  body to the seat facing while seated; build a want/root/body/station facing probe before
  touching it ‚Äî do not guess rotations on a host that cannot render.**
- **Guessing rotations blind is a trap all by itself.** Attempt 3 shipped, in order: a 180¬∞ flip,
  a `gambit_terry_face` degrees lever, then a *per-frame* facing reapply ‚Äî the last of which
  derived a networked-visible rotation locally on every machine and desynced host vs client (one
  90¬∞ off the other). Only a diagnostic dump ended it. **Same rule as "build the live-values dump
  on day one": a facing/pose value you cannot see must be MEASURED, never dialled by guess.**

## Build order ‚Äî one editor session per phase, judged before the next is written

- **Phase 0 ‚Äî the seated pose, signed off in the editor (NEW, from attempt 3).** No hand code at
  all. Fix the three seated-body defects and prove them with a diagnostic before anything else:
  (a) the render body ‚Üí seat facing (re-assert the `Body` child to the seat facing while seated;
  ship a want/root/body/station `gambit_terry` probe first), (b) `SeatSitBack` back to `36`,
  (c) confirm a plain seated terry ‚Äî approached from any angle, on host AND a joined client ‚Äî
  sits square on its chair facing the board. **Attempt 3 never got past here; attempt 4 must land
  this and get the owner's sign-off before writing a line of Phase 1.**
- **Phase 1 ‚Äî near moves, the whole feature in miniature.** Seated only, no half-rise. Rest
  anchor ‚Üí on a committed move by a seated seat: hand travels to the origin piece (Approach),
  the piece then slides with the hand tracking it (Carry, flat), grip opens and the hand returns
  to the anchor (Release). Fixed pinch. **Prove it reads as a hand moving a piece for reachable
  squares before touching anything else.** Everyone sees it, including the mover.
- **Phase 2 ‚Äî far reach.** Add the half-rise, MOVE-ONLY, so the arm reaches far pieces;
  truly-unreachable ‚Üí hand trails, piece finishes alone. The attempt-2 planner (`HalfRise.cs`,
  harness-proven) and servo are on `origin/m14-terry-halfrise-ik` for the *measurements* ‚Äî
  **re-derive, don't restore files.**
- **Phase 3 ‚Äî captures, minimal.** Attacker move as Phase 1/2; the taken piece slides to its
  owner's tray on its own, no hand, no exchange beat.
- **Phase 4 ‚Äî polish.** The tempo slider, the rest-anchor position, grip height/offset, the
  elbow roll and forearm-derived pitch (facts #2). Optionally re-derive the **spectator mirror**
  (so the room can SEE a lichess game being played by hands) ‚Äî it worked on the branch; it is
  independent of the arm mechanics.

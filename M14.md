# M14 — a seated terry reaches out and moves the piece by hand

> **Read this as HISTORY, not instructions.** M14 has been attempted **three times and
> abandoned three times**. Everything in this file except *The goal* (the owner's intent) is an
> **account of what each attempt tried, why, and how it went** — not a set of proven facts to
> copy. Several confident "facts" from earlier attempts turned out **wrong** (called out inline —
> e.g. the seat-setback value). So treat every engineering claim below as a **lead to re-verify
> in the editor**, attributed to the attempt that made it, never as settled truth.
>
> **Why the caveat is this strong:** the dev host **cannot render or run engine code**. Nobody who
> wrote these notes ever *saw* the thing they described — it was only ever judged by the owner in
> the editor, one screenshot at a time, and that round-trip is where every "obvious" fix died. The
> only things provable on this host are **Sandbox-free math in a dotnet harness**; everything
> visual is a guess until the owner looks.

## The goal (owner intent — THIS part is the spec, pinned, don't relitigate)

A seated terry **reaches out and moves the piece with its hand**, like a person playing chess.
M13 shipped the seated bodies (gated behind `ChessRing.TerrySeated`); this adds the working
(right) arm. Chess is one-handed — right hand only, always. The owner's pinned design:

1. **The hand moves the piece.** The people move the pieces; the hand is the agent, the piece is
   what it moves. Every ambiguity resolves toward "what would a hand do."
2. **Move-only hand.** The hand animates ONLY for a committed move. While *deciding* (hovering,
   selecting) it does **not** track the cursor and does **not** reach — it rests. (This deletes
   the whole hover-tempo problem.)
3. **Anchored rest.** Between moves the arm holds one fixed composed rest pose (forearm/hand off
   to the side, not on the board) via a light IK anchor — a consistent spot every move starts and
   ends at. Not released to the raw sit animation.
4. **Flat slide, hand follows the piece.** For v1 the piece stays on the board surface and slides
   (no lift-off arc); the hand rides just above it. A lift can come later.
5. **Half-rise is allowed but MOVE-ONLY.** For a piece past the seated arm's reach the terry may
   rise/lean off the chair — but ONLY while executing a move, never on hover.
6. **Far, still-unreachable → the hand trails.** If even the risen arm can't reach, the hand
   follows the piece as far as it goes and the piece finishes its slide alone.
7. **Minimal captures (v1).** Attacker moves as any move; the taken piece slides to its owner's
   tray on its own — no hand, no exchange beat. (A fuller choreography the owner specified is
   recorded under Attempt 2 as a *someday* idea, not v1.)
8. **Fixed pinch grip.** One canned pinch pose while on a piece. No per-finger work in v1.
9. **Everyone sees the hand, including the mover.** No per-viewer special-casing.
10. **Move tempo is ONE tunable, ~0.8–1s rest-to-rest**, deliberate not hurried; and every tuning
    knob applies to ALL terries at once, not just the local one.

## Why this is hard — what Attempt 1 concluded (and its numbers, to re-verify)

Attempt 1 (the "reaching hand", branch since deleted) set out to just IK the seated hand onto the
piece, measured the geometry live in the editor, and concluded **a fixed-size seated citizen
cannot reach across this board.** Its measurements (live editor, White seat, station-local — the
author called them "the authority" *but this host can't render, so re-verify*):

- **Working arm ≈ 20u** (`arm_upper_R → arm_lower_R1 → hand_R1`, two-bone IK). Attempt 1 read the
  citizen animgraph in `sbox-public` and found **no stretch/soften anywhere on the active arm
  path** — the chain root is the shoulder, pinned by the spine, so a target past ~20u just
  straightens the two bones and the hand stops short. (Believed solid; it's the load-bearing
  claim under the whole half-rise, so worth re-confirming first.)
- **Board is 34u deep**, near rank ≈ x−17, far rank ≈ x+17, centred at x=0. Seated shoulder sits
  ~15u back from the near edge, so the honest reach ceiling was about **rank 2** — half of
  White's *own* back rank wasn't reachable, the far ranks were 30–35u short.
- **A seated idle's only lean is a ~5° spine clamp ≈ 2u** (`aim_body`, clamped in the idle
  upper-body aim node; the 80–90° pitch nodes are *weapon-aim* states, not the seated idle).
- Attempt 1's escape hatches, both flagged risky/unverified: **(a)** override a spine bone every
  frame with `SetBoneTransform`/`SetBoneOverride` to *fake* a big lean (moves the whole upper-body
  subtree incl. the IK root) — "does it compose with hand IK in the same frame?" was **never
  verified**; **(b)** shrink the board to the arm — but reaching your *own half* needs the board
  at **~¼ size**, across needs **~⅒**, and it's a shared board centred at x=0 that can't be biased
  toward one seat, and rescaling cascades into the seat cameras, ring layout, engaged-UI rect and
  the spectator board (each has its own scale) — milestone-sized, and it makes a doll's board.
- Useful bone names it left behind (re-verify): eye bones are `eye_L`/`eye_R` (not `eyes`); spine
  is `pelvis, spine_0..2, neck_0, head`; sit poses are a `sit` enum, `ChairForward = sitting_02` —
  **whether `sit=2` leans the torso over the table is in the binary clip and was never readable on
  this host; Attempt 1 flagged "editor check worth doing" and nobody has.**

**Takeaway Attempt 1 handed forward:** reaching is impossible seated *unless the pelvis leaves the
chair* — which became Attempt 2's whole premise.

## The three attempts — approach, reasoning, outcome

### Attempt 1 — the reaching hand *(branch deleted; nothing survives but the numbers above)*
Approach: IK the seated hand onto the piece, tune with reach knobs. Outcome: **abandoned** on the
reach proof above. A trap it documented: a "reach clamp" (pull far targets onto a reach sphere)
made a diagnostic grid read all-reachable **because it measured reach to the clamped target** — the
hand parked at ~rank 2 for every far piece and never moved. A frozen thing that *looks* satisfied.

### Attempt 2 — the IK half-rise *(the one that mechanically WORKED)*
Approach: when a square is past the leaned arm, **half-rise** — override the pelvis bone
up-and-forward toward the piece (the same subtree-carry `SetBoneTransform` trick, bounded by the
*legs* not the chair), feet planted via the engine's `foot_left`/`foot_right` IK, off-hand bracing
the tabletop via `hand_left`, and the moved piece **riding the rendered `hand_R` bone**.

**Outcome: it was a working, networked MVP — abandoned for the LOOK, not the mechanics.** Reach,
pick-up, the spectator mirror (the room sees a lichess game played by hands), and inspector tuning
were all *verified across two clients*. The owner's verdict was still "looks like a COMPLETELY
BUSTED GAME," and rather than grind the polish, Attempt 3 restarted from scratch. So: if the
half-rise is ever needed again, **the hard networked mechanics were already solved here** — the
open problem was always the *look*. What Attempt 2 believed/measured (re-verify):

- **The trick that let 4 IK chains coexist with bone overrides:** the animgraph solves IK *before*
  overrides apply, so aim each IK target at **(true target − the override translation its chain
  will ride)** — feet ride the pelvis, hands ride pelvis+spine. Translation-only, so it's a vector
  subtraction. (Attempt 2 claimed overrides landed "to the decimal.")
- **A residual ~5u native warp** survived every override (procedural/twist bones, unreadable via
  any API). Attempt 2's answer was a **closed-loop servo**: measure last frame's rendered-hand vs
  the true ask, integrate, clamp, decay — "don't model the unmodellable, steer it out."
- **Harness reach: 64/64 squares** with the *live-measured* skeleton (pelvis z ≈ 16.6, **not** the
  guessed 23 — the lower pelvis buys the leg triangle headroom), `MaxLean 12`, `RiseLift 0.3`,
  `SeatSitBack 36`, and **CHOSEN foot plants** (pelvis + a small forward step), *never* the
  animated ankles — the sit pose tucks them ~25u back and spends the whole leg budget. Three
  things earlier cuts got wrong: the rise must be **mostly horizontal** (leg budget is scarce);
  the foot step must be **exact** leg-triangle arithmetic; the rise search is a **descending scan**
  because the foot-plate clamp makes feasibility **non-monotone** in the corners.
- **The owner's ranked jank list at the pause** (the bar was "stop looking broken", clipping OK):
  (1) **bent elbow** — the reach margin was cranked to 8, pulling every ask deep inside the reach
  sphere so the arm never extends; with the servo truing the hand it was expected to drop to ~2–3
  (retest that one slider first). (2) **kinked wrist** — a *fixed* 60° hand pitch; it needs to
  derive from the forearm's actual bearing. (3) tempo.
- **Torso yaw shipped as the one authored rotation** (`gambit_terry_yaw`, capped ~30°, eased in
  with the rise, written on the spine override) — two-bone IK can't turn the chest. It was called
  "safe" *because* the servo trues the fingertip regardless of whether override rotation
  propagates to the arm subtree (**that propagation was never proven**). Attempt 2 warned: don't
  extend the same trick to torso *pitch* — pitch moves head/camera mass.
- **Finger leads it never finished:** the `FingerAdjustment_{L|R}{1-5}_{Bend|Curl|Roll|Spread}`
  procedural params (floats −60..60) are documented on the *first-person arms* graph and were
  **unverified on the third-person citizen graph**; the fallback was per-phalanx overrides à la the
  engine's `VrHand` (each phalanx needs its own override, since override *rotations* don't carry
  children).
- **Two directions it left open, both unbuilt:** authored animation clips via
  `DirectPlayback.Play(name, target, heading, interpTime)` (coarse board map, uniform grab height,
  fingers may clip — trades precision for motion quality); and a fuller **capture choreography** the
  owner specified — pick up the *attacker* first, both pieces briefly share the square, then a
  single continuous *DropAndSwap* beat (real duration, zero dwell — the hand never rests between
  placing one piece and leaving with the other) carries the victim to the tray, with the grip
  morphing thumb+index pinch → index-extended thumb+middle. Recorded as a *someday* idea; v1
  captures are minimal (design point 7).

### Attempt 3 — the one-clock model *(never got past the chair)*
Approach (the owner's pinned reconciliation of "the hand moves the piece" with "parent the IK
target to the piece"): **one clock owns a move.** The PIECE's slide is the authoritative path
(authored by `ChessBoardView`, lands exactly on the square); the HAND's IK target is *derived from
the piece every frame* (`hand_right` = `piece + gripOffset`), so the flaky arm can only ever be a
little off the *right* answer, never authoritative. One timeline, three phases: **Approach** (piece
frozen at origin, hand travels to it), **Carry** (piece slides, hand tracks it), **Release** (grip
opens, hand eases to rest). The piece doesn't move until Approach *ends* — a **timeline gate, not a
proximity test**, which was meant to kill Attempt 2's "grab never fired" class of bug for good.

**Outcome: the hand model was built and proven green in a harness (`MoveGesture` timeline +
`HandReach` over 64 squares) but NEVER ONCE evaluated in the editor** — the entire session was
eaten by *seated-body* problems that have nothing to do with hands, every one invisible on this
host and found by the owner one screenshot at a time. So the one-clock model remains **unproven in
practice.** What ate the session:

- **The seated body faced the wrong way — and the cause is an M13 bug, not a hand bug.** The
  citizen `SkinnedModelRenderer` lives on a **`Body` child** GO whose yaw is driven by
  `PlayerController.OnRotateRenderBody`. `BeginEngage` **disables the controller, then**
  `PlantOnSeat` rotates only the **root** — so the render body *freezes at whatever heading you
  were walking when you pressed E*. Walk up head-on and it looks fine; approach from the side and
  the body sits visibly turned while the root correctly faces the board. **Latent on master** (same
  `BeginEngage` order); it only shows once someone studies a seated body from outside, which M14 is
  the first thing to do. A `gambit_terry` facing probe (want/root/body/station yaws) is what finally
  isolated it — `root` was right, `body` was ~55° off. *Suggested* fix: re-assert the render body to
  the seat facing while seated. **The lesson that cost the most: don't guess rotations on a host
  that can't render** — Attempt 3 shipped a 180° flip, then a degrees lever, then a *per-frame*
  reapply that derived a networked-visible rotation locally on every machine and *desynced host vs
  client* (one 90° off), before the probe found the truth. **Measure first, guess never.**
- **The seat setback is wrong on master, and the old note about it was WRONG.** `SeatSitBack` is
  **`26`** on master right now — the abandoned Attempt-1 reach leftover (scooted forward from `36`
  to drag rank 1 under the reaching hand) that was **never reverted**. At `26` the seated chest is
  buried ~a third into the tabletop (invisible from this host and the seated player's own
  first-person view). An earlier version of this doc *confidently claimed master was `36` and said
  "do not scoot"* — it was flat wrong. `36` is the documented centre of the working band; a fourth
  attempt should set it back **and actually change the code, not just the doc.**
- **Two trivial-but-invisible placement bugs:** the rest anchor was authored *on* the board
  (x −16, vs the board's near edge at x ≈ −19.5); `holdtype=holditem` was forced every frame at
  rest so the idle hand gripped an invisible object. Obvious in a screenshot, unprovable here.

**The meta-lesson, three times running: the killer is never the hand math — it is seated-body /
room state invisible on this host.** A fourth attempt should get a correct, diagnostic-backed
seated pose (facing + setback + rest anchor) landed and *signed off by the owner in the editor*
BEFORE writing a line of hand code.

## Engine observations from the attempts — re-verify before trusting

Read from `sbox-public` and/or measured live during attempts 1–2. Kept because re-deriving from
zero is expensive — but at least one "verified" claim here has already proven wrong, so confirm:

1. **Only four game-facing IK chains:** `hand_left`, `hand_right`, `foot_left`, `foot_right` —
   each a two-bone solve to the wrist/ankle via `SetIk(name, worldTransform)`. **No finger IK
   anywhere** — fingers are *posed*, never *solved*.
2. **The hand IK consumes the target ROTATION, and it drives the ELBOW pole.** Consequences the
   attempts measured: **roll** the hand target to swing the elbow *out* of the torso (roll 0 traps
   the arm in a vertical plane — the "t-rex arm"); derive hand **pitch** from the forearm's actual
   bearing, not a fixed value (a fixed nose-down pitch hyper-flexed the wrist on a flat reach — the
   "kinked wrist").
3. **Override TRANSLATIONS carry the whole bone subtree exactly; override ROTATIONS do NOT carry
   child bones** (measured: ~15.8u of shoulder travel budgeted from a rotation, ~3u materialised).
   So per-bone finger posing needs an override *per phalanx* (what `VrHand` does).
4. **The animgraph solves IK BEFORE bone overrides apply** — pre-compensate any IK target by the
   override translation its chain rides. A residual ~5u native warp remains; Attempt 2 used a servo.
5. **`SetIk` wants a WORLD transform** (it does `WorldTransform.ToLocal` internally) — hand it a
   local one and the hand parks near the floor.
6. **The citizen `SkinnedModelRenderer` must live on a `Body` CHILD GO**, never the controller's
   own GO (the animator welds the player to world origin otherwise). *(This is also the source of
   Attempt 3's facing bug — that child's yaw is controller-driven and freezes on sit.)*
7. **Seat setback: master ships `SeatSitBack = 26`, which is TOO FAR FORWARD** (chest in the
   table). `36` is the documented working centre. *(Corrected — the prior "master is 36, do not
   scoot" claim was wrong.)*
8. **Tuning must be GLOBAL across every terry** (owner requirement). The pattern: pose/tempo knobs
   are process **statics**; a scene component **pushes** them on change; every terry's driver reads
   the same statics, so one slider moves all terries. **And build the live-values console dump on
   day one** — two full editor sessions across the attempts were lost tuning sliders that were
   confirmed live and changed nothing visible, because a different subsystem dominated the motion.
9. **The dev host cannot render or run engine code.** Put any real geometry in `Code/Chess/` with
   no engine dependency (its own tiny vector type, no `Vector3`) so it can be proven in a dotnet
   harness — the only proof available here. (Same doctrine as `BoardDiff`, `CapturedMaterial`,
   `MoveSpeech`.)

## If someone attempts a fourth time — what the failures suggest (not a mandate)

- **Phase 0 — the seated pose, signed off in the editor, before any hand code.** No hands at all.
  Fix the render-body → seat facing (ship the want/root/body/station probe first), set
  `SeatSitBack` back to `36`, and confirm a plain seated terry — approached from *any* angle, on
  host AND a joined client — sits square on its chair facing the board. Attempt 3 never cleared
  this; it is the thing to land and get owner sign-off on first.
- **Phase 1 — near moves only**, the one-clock model (design points 1–4, 8–10), seated, no rise.
  Prove it reads as a hand moving a piece for reachable squares before anything else.
- **Phase 2 — far reach**, the move-only half-rise (design points 5–6). Attempt 2 solved the
  mechanics; its numbers and jank list above are the starting point — but re-verify, and remember
  its own verdict was that the *look* was the hard part.
- **Phase 3 — captures, minimal** (design point 7). The fuller choreography is a someday idea.
- **Kill chain, keep it total:** `ChessRing.TerrySeated` (M13 switch) → a hands sub-switch →
  a half-rise sub-switch. All off = the shipped M13 world (bodies, no hands), a clean revert.
- **An alternative worth a spike if procedural reach keeps reading broken:** authored clips
  (`DirectPlayback`), per Attempt 2.

# M13 — Seated terries

**Two people sitting at a table playing chess.** Chairs at both seats, terries in them, and
hands that hover, hesitate and reach for the piece the player is actually thinking about —
mirrored honestly to the other side.

This file is the milestone's design and its evidence. It is not a checklist; it exists because
**this host cannot render, compile or run any of it**, so every number here was derived rather
than seen, and the derivation is the only thing standing between the design and a placement bug
that ships. Facts marked **[GUESS]** rest on the sit pose, which is unknowable here. Everything
else was read from `sbox-public` or `client/Code/` at the path cited.

When M13 ships, the parts of this worth keeping move into `CLAUDE.md` and the rest goes to git.

---

## The starting truth

**Nobody has ever sat down.** `LobbyPlayer.BeginEngage` teleports the citizen to the seat spot
**keeping its own standing Z** and yaws it at the board, so a "seated" player is a citizen
standing bolt upright against the table edge. It has been that way since the fork.

Nobody noticed because `HideLocalAvatar()` disables every renderer on your own avatar the moment
you sit and re-asserts it every frame. **You have never seen yourself play**, and the person
opposite you has been standing the whole time.

`HideLocalAvatar`'s own doc comment already says *"never a remote/proxy one — those stay visible,
which M13 leans on"*. That is the seam this milestone was written against.

## The two findings that reshaped the milestone

### 1. "Only draw the arms" is impossible, and the frame makes it unnecessary

`sbox-public/game/addons/citizen/Assets/models/citizen/prefabs/citizen_bodygrouplist.vmdl_prefab`
defines **exactly five** bodygroups — `Head`, `Chest`, `Legs`, `Hands`, `Feet` — each with choice
0 = meshes and choice 1 = `[ ]`.

**There is no arms mesh. The arms are inside `Torso_LOD0..3`, i.e. the `Chest` group.**
(`grep -rali 'arm_lod\|arms_lod'` over the citizen addon returns nothing.) So:

- `SetBodyGroup("Chest", 1)` takes the arms with it. Body groups cannot isolate them.
- **Bone zero-scaling cannot either** — the arms are children of the spine chain, so collapsing
  the torso collapses the arms. It is unprecedented in `sbox-public`, and its only undo is
  `ClearPhysicsBones()`, which is all-or-nothing and misleadingly named: it maps to
  `SceneModel.ClearBoneOverrides()` (`SkinnedModelRenderer.Bones.cs:180-184`), nothing to do with
  ragdolls.

**API trap:** only `Head`'s choices carry `name = "on"/"off"`. The other four groups'
`BodyGroupChoice` nodes have **no name field**, so `SetBodyGroup("Chest","off")` matches nothing
and silently does nothing. Use `SetBodyGroup(string, int)` with `1` = off.

**And then the arithmetic made the whole problem go away.** At the default anchor the camera sits
at station-local `(−32.12, 0, 80.12)` aimed 55° + `SeatLookDownAngle` 8° = **63° depression**.
`FieldOfView` is 75 **horizontal**, so at 16:9 the vertical half-FOV is
`atan(tan(37.5°) × 9/16)` = **23.35°**, putting the **bottom edge of frame** at **86.35°** below
horizontal:

| body part | its x | frame bottom edge at that z | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| head front (z=49) | −30.50 | −30.13 | **out of frame by 0.37** [GUESS] |
| shoulder front (z=44) | −29.00 | −29.81 | in frame by 0.81 [GUESS] |
| hand over e2 (z=38.25) | −12.19 | −29.45 | in frame by 17.26 |

**The shot the milestone asks for — forearms and hands entering from the bottom edge, reaching
onto the board, nothing else — is what the default anchor already gives, for free.** The bottom
edge lands at x ≈ −29.5, essentially the tabletop edge (−30). Not a coincidence: the anchor was
tuned so the board fills the frame.

The head also does **not** occlude the board — the camera→near-board-edge ray passes x = −23.9 at
head height, 11.6 inboard of the head at −35.5.

So arms-only needs no mechanism. Only the head is marginal (0.37, inside the error bars), and
`SetBodyGroup("Head", "off")` is the one trim worth making.

> **The limit this implies, stated plainly:** as `SeatEyeBlend` (below) descends toward the eyes,
> more torso enters frame and **there is no clean mechanism to remove it**. You cannot dial to 0
> and keep arms-only. Combined with the occlusion table below, **blend ≈ 0.6–1.0 is the usable
> band.**

### 2. An eye-level camera destroys the board

A piece of height `h` at view-pitch `t` occludes `h/tan(t)` of board behind it. The board is 8
cells = 39.0 world deep; `PieceScale` = 1.5, so a king is 9.6 tall and a pawn 4.8.

| camera | far-rank pitch | pawn hides | king hides |
|---|---|---|---|
| eye, sitting upright (−48, 48) | 13.6° | 4.1 sq | **8.1 sq** |
| eye, leaning in (−40, 54) | 20.9° | 2.6 sq | 5.2 sq |
| eye + 15u lift (−44, 63) | 26.7° | 2.0 sq | 3.9 sq |
| **CURRENT anchor (−32.1, 80.1)** | **44.2°** | 1.0 sq | 2.0 sq |

**At true eye height a king hides the entire board behind it.** The current anchor is playable
precisely because it is 44° to the far rank. The camera therefore becomes eye-anchored and
tunable, but **its default reproduces today's anchor bit-for-bit** (proof below).

### A third finding, retracted — worth keeping so nobody re-finds it

An earlier pass of this document claimed **the engine never animates Gambit's citizens**, on the
evidence that `lobby.scene:251` has `"Renderer": null`, nothing in `client/Code/` assigns
`PlayerController.Renderer`, and the engine's only call site is guarded:
`if ( UseAnimatorControls && Renderer.IsValid() ) UpdateAnimation( Renderer );`
(`PlayerController.DefaultControls.cs:59-62`).

**That was wrong. `Renderer` self-heals.** `PlayerController.Elements.cs:35-37`:

```csharp
if ( Renderer is null && UseAnimatorControls )
    Renderer = GetComponentInChildren<SkinnedModelRenderer>();
```

called from `EnsureComponentsCreated()` at `PlayerController.cs:139` and `:181`. Our scene has
`UseAnimatorControls: true`, so it resolves to `Body` at runtime. The engine animates our citizens
normally, `BeginEngage`'s comment about the walk cycle looping is **correct**, and its four
`move_*` stomps do real work.

Recorded because the wrong version was reached by reading three files that all *looked* decisive,
and the right one lives in a fourth. **A guarded call site is not evidence the guard fails.**

## The chair

A **cantilever tube frame** — Breuer/Cesca. Legs a U opening backwards, arms a U opening
forwards, sharing the seat rail so that in side elevation they read as **an S rotated 90°**;
square seat pad; L-shaped back; frame a thin tube.

The two descriptions cross-check each other, which is the design's own proof: *side elevation* is
one 5-segment polyline (floor rail → front riser → seat rail → back riser → armrest), and in
*plan* the floor sled closes at the front while the back-riser cross-tube closes at the back.
Both readings land on the same object.

```
Seat pad     centre x = −32.12 (the walk-up spot), 18 deep (X) × 18 wide (Y)
             spans x −41.12 .. −23.12 ; top surface z = 18.00
Tube radius  0.75
Side frames  at y = ±9.00, each a 5-segment polyline in x–z:
  1 floor rail   z = 0.75    x −41.12 → −23.12
  2 front riser  x = −23.12  z 0.75 → 17.40
  3 seat rail    z = 17.40   x −23.12 → −41.12
  4 back riser   x = −41.12  z 17.40 → 24.75
  5 armrest      z = 24.75   x −41.12 → −23.12   (front end free = U opens forward)
Cross-tubes  front floor cross  x = −23.12, z = 0.75,  y −9 → +9
             back rail cross    x = −41.12, z = 17.40, y −9 → +9
Seat panel   box x −41.12..−23.12, y ±9, z 16.80..18.00   (inset; rail spans 16.65..18.15)
Back panel   upright x = −41.12, z 22.0 → 34.0, y ±9
             return  z = 34.0,  x −41.12 → −38.0
```

**The armrest height is not a style choice — the tabletop's underside sets it.** This is the
`ClockPlateHeight` / `ClockPlaneOriginZ` lesson again, and the number proves it:

```
ArmrestZ = TableTopSlabBottom − ArmrestTableGap − TubeRadius
         = 27.0 − 1.5 − 0.75 = 24.75      → armrest top 25.50, clearance 1.50 ✔
```

Typed as "8 above the seat" (z = 26) it tops out at **26.75 against an underside of 27.00 — a
0.25 clearance.** Derive it; do not type it.

Every clearance, checked at the **edges**, not the centres:

| pair | clearance |
|---|---|
| front riser (x −23.12) vs Foot plate (x ±15) | 8.12 ✔ |
| seat pad (z 16.8..18) vs Top slab (z 27..30) | 9.00 ✔ |
| armrest (top 25.5) vs Top slab bottom (27) | 1.50 ✔ |

### Where it lives

- **Parent: `station`** — a sibling of `WhiteAnchor` / `Table` / `BoardPlaque` / `TableClock`,
  matching `BuildStationPlaque` and `BuildStationClock`. Named **`"Chair White"` / `"Chair Black"`**,
  mirroring `"Tray White"`. Not under `Table`, so `ChessBoardView.ResolveCells`' `"Cell "`
  prefix-scan can never see it.
- **Built inside `ChessRing`** — which buys hotload for free: `Editor/HotloadRebuild.cs` already
  calls `ring.RebuildPreview()`. A separate builder component would need its own registration.
- **Always present at both seats.** A table with no chairs reads as a table you cannot sit at, and
  spawning on sit would pop a chair in under you.
- **No collider.** The consequence is real and accepted: **you walk through every chair in the
  room.** In exchange `SeatWorldPosition` and `InteractRange` (90, scene-overridden at
  `lobby.scene:274`) are untouched, the ring's floor-slide keeps working, and CLAUDE.md's "never a
  `BoxCollider` on a non-uniformly-scaled GO" trap never arises.

### Tuck and pull-out

The instinct "sitting pulls the chair out" needs one reconciliation: at the seated position the
terry's hips are at x = −36 and hand reach (~29) gets to x ≈ −7, past the near rank at −17.06.
Pull the chair *further* out and the terry cannot reach the board. So:

- **Seated position = the geometry above.** This *is* the pulled-out state.
- **Tucked = translated `ChairTuckInset` toward the board** when the seat is unoccupied.

`ChairTuckInset` is bounded by the Foot plate, not free:

```
front riser x = −23.12 must stay outboard of the Foot plate edge (−15)
→ ChairTuckInset ≤ 8.12.   Use 6.0 → tucked front x = −17.12, clear by 2.12 ✔
```

**Stretch goal, and nearly free:** lerp `LocalPosition.x` between tucked and seated over ~0.5 s on
an occupancy change. It derives from `[Sync]` occupancy, so every client animates it identically
with **zero new networking**. ~8 lines.

### Construction

**A lathe cannot bend a tube** — it revolves about one axis. Build the frame as **straight tube
segments with a sphere at each vertex** to hide the mitre:

- Add `ChessSetBuilder.BuildTube(parent, name, Vector3 a, Vector3 b, float radius, Color tint)`,
  backed by one **cached** Z-axis cylinder from the existing `BuildLathe(name, profile)` (a
  rectangle profile revolved *is* a cylinder). Cache beside `_latheCache`.
- Vertices use `AddSphere(parent, name, pos, radius*2, tint)` — already exists at
  `ChessSetBuilder.cs:274`.
- **Every segment is a pair of endpoints in station-local space, so every edge is verifiable on
  this host.** That is the whole reason to prefer it over a swept mesh — CLAUDE.md's *"nothing
  here can render, so check where the EDGES land"*.

> ⚠️ **The one thing that cannot be checked here:** `Rotation.LookAt` makes **+X** forward, but the
> lathe cylinder's axis is **+Z**. The mapping is `Rotation.LookAt(b−a, Vector3.Up) *
> Rotation.FromPitch(90f)`. A wrong mapping puts every tube at 90° to where it belongs. It is the
> **first** item on the editor checklist because everything else is invisible until it is right.

**D5 fallback hook**, mirroring `BuildPiece` and `PieceHeight`'s documented convention (*"the model
path scales its mesh to the same height so a future import keeps the board's proportions"*):
`Model.Load("models/chess/chair.vmdl")` → one GO + `ModelRenderer` scaled so the model's height
becomes `ChairHeight` (34.0); else the procedural frame.

### Colour

- **Panels (seat + back) = `Gambit.UI.WallTheme.Accent`** — the room theme, i.e. "world colour".
  It reads `PlayerData.Load()?.WorldLightColor` and **already falls back** to
  `DefaultAccent = #606060` on AUTO/empty (`WallTheme.cs:36-41`). Reuse it; do not re-implement the
  fallback.
  - `Gambit.World → Gambit.UI` is already established (`ChessRing` constructs
    `StationScreenPanel` and `TableClockTextPanel`). Do not move the getter.
  - **New precedent worth a CLAUDE.md line:** `WallTheme`'s own comment says *"The cabinet UI is
    intentionally NOT themed from this"*. Themed **world geometry** is new. It is defensible — a
    chair is furniture and the room theme is a room-tuning knob exactly like voice range — but
    without the line, the next reader finds the comment and concludes the chair is a mistake.
- **Frame = 75 % toward the piece colour**, against `ChessSetBuilder.WhiteColor = (0.85, 0.81,
  0.72)` / `BlackColor = (0.09, 0.07, 0.06)`:
  `Color.Lerp(midGrey, pieceColor, ChairFrameTintStrength = 0.75f)`
  → White **(0.7625, 0.7325, 0.665)**, Black **(0.1925, 0.1775, 0.17)**.
  A bare `pieceColor * 0.75` was rejected: it drives Black to (0.068, 0.053, 0.045) —
  indistinguishable from the table's own wood at `(0.16, 0.11, 0.07)` under the 3.3× table spot.
  *(This is an interpretation of "75% black vs white", not a derivation — hence the exposed
  constant.)*
- **Re-tint on change.** `WorldLightColor` changes at runtime from the settings board.
  **`World/MarqueeGlow.cs:26-35` is the precedent** (closer than `RoomLightOrbit`): it polls
  `SettingsModel.SettingsVersion` in `OnUpdate` and re-applies on change. Copy it.
- **Per-client tint is the doctrine working, not a bug** — stations are network-spawned but
  everything cosmetic is local and rebuilt per client.

## Sitting the terry

The engine's `BaseChair` / `SitMoveMode` path is **unavailable**: it needs a live
`PlayerController`, and `BeginEngage` disables ours. We write the params ourselves — which is what
`BaseChair.UpdatePlayerAnimator` does anyway (lines 235-239).

**Do not add `CitizenAnimationHelper`.** It is a thin `Target.Set(...)` wrapper that does not
expose the finger param we need and would fight `PlayerController` while roaming. Write directly to
`_bodyRenderer` — the exact API `BeginEngage` already uses.

```csharp
r.Set( "sit", 1 );                   // AnimatorSitPose.Chair ≡ SittingStyle.Chair1
r.Set( "sit_offset_height", h );     // RAW units. BaseChair passes SitHeight*12 → domain ±12
r.Set( "b_sit", true );              // distinct from "sit"; BaseChair never touches it
r.Set( "b_grounded", true ); r.Set( "b_climbing", false );
r.Set( "b_swim", false );  r.Set( "duck", 0f );
```

> **`BaseChair.AnimatorSitPose` and `CitizenAnimationHelper.SittingStyle` are two enums writing the
> SAME `"sit"` int**, with different names for identical values (`None`≡`Standing`=0,
> `Chair1`≡`Chair`=1, `Chair2`≡`ChairForward`=2, …). `BaseChair`'s names are the better ones.
> `BaseChair` scales `SitHeight × 12`; the helper passes raw. And `"sit"` (pose int) is **not**
> `"b_sit"` (bool). Four ways to be quietly wrong about one pose.

`citizen_sit.vsubgrph` carries `sit_offset_height` with `+12/−12/+3` nodes, confirming the ×12
domain. It also holds a node named *"Disable global feet IK (done over 200ms)"* — sitting turns
feet IK off for us.

### The transform

`BeginEngage` keeps its **standing z** and plants at `SeatWorldPosition` (|x| = 32.12). Both change.

Add **`ChessStation.SeatSitWorldPosition(seat)`** → station-local `(side * SeatSitBack, 0,
SeatSitZ)`. Defaults **`SeatSitBack = 36.0`**, **`SeatSitZ = 18.0`** (the seat pad's top surface).

| check | value |
|---|---|
| hips at −36.0 vs pad centre −32.12 | 3.9 back of centre; pad back edge −41.12 ✔ (this is how a chair works) |
| **belly front −30.5 vs tabletop edge −30.0** | **clears by 0.5** [GUESS] |
| knees −19.5 vs Pedestal (−7.5) | 12.0 ✔ |
| knees vs Foot plate (−15) | 4.5 ✔ |
| thigh z 18 vs tabletop underside 27 | 9.0 ✔ |

**That 0.5 is why the hips sit back of the pad centre.** Hips at the pad centre (−32.12) put the
belly at −26.6 — **3.4 inside the tabletop slab**.

> **The constraint nobody specified, and the one that pins the chair:** the elbows-on-table idle
> only works with hips at **x ≈ −34…−38**. At −42 the elbow reach is −32.0 and at −45 it is −35.0
> — both **off the table edge (−30)**, so the idle becomes physically impossible. `SeatSitBack = 36`
> sits in the middle of the only band that works.

**Leave `SeatWorldPosition` alone.** It is the walk-up/proximity spot and `FindNearbySeat` reads it.
Two different questions, two methods.

Update the sites that plant the body: `BeginEngage`, `InstantReseat`, and `UpdateSeatSwitch`'s
end-snap — which currently does `seatPos.z = _switchAvatarFrom.z`, correct **by accident** once z is
seated. Make it explicit via `SeatSitWorldPosition` or it pops 1.9 units the day someone changes
`SeatSitBack`.

### Authority: nobody's, and that is the answer

**The pose is not networked.** The driver runs on **every client for every station**, resolves each
seat's avatar from the `[Sync(FromHost)]` occupancy, and writes the params locally. Same doctrine as
clothing. What *is* networked is the **avatar transform**, owner→everyone, which `BeginEngage`
already relies on.

> ⚠️ **Editor check:** a proxy's `PlayerController` is **still enabled** (`LobbyPlayer.OnUpdate`
> early-returns on `IsProxy` before disabling it), and `PlayerController.cs:156` writes
> `Renderer.WorldRotation` from `EyeAngles`. Confirm that does not fight the seated pose on remote
> clients. Probably benign — Terries look fine standing at tables today — but unverified against a
> *sit* pose.

## The camera

### `SeatEyeBlend`, default 1.0

A `[Property, Range(0,1)] float SeatEyeBlend = 1f` on `ChessRing`, lerping **eye position → today's
orbit position**, then deriving the rotation from the blended position with the existing rule:

```csharp
Vector3 eye   = new Vector3( side * SeatEyeBack, 0, SeatEyeHeight );   // 36.0, 49.0  [GUESS]
Vector3 orbit = center + new Vector3( side*R*cos(pitch), 0, R*sin(pitch) );
Vector3 pos   = Vector3.Lerp( eye, orbit, SeatEyeBlend );
anchor.LocalPosition = pos;
anchor.LocalRotation = Rotation.LookAt( center - pos, Vector3.Up ) * Rotation.FromPitch( SeatLookDownAngle );
```

**Proof the default equals today, exactly:** at blend = 1, `Vector3.Lerp(eye, orbit, 1) === orbit`,
and the rotation line is **character-for-character** `BuildSeatAnchor`'s existing one. So the
default is `(±32.12, 0, 80.12)` with rotation `LookAt(center−pos, Up) * FromPitch(8°)` —
**bit-for-bit today's output**, not "near enough".

Blending the **position only, never the rotation** is what buys this: the camera aims at the board
at every blend value, and there is one formula rather than two.

The alternative (re-express the orbit anchor as eye + lift/back) is **disqualified**: the eye
position depends on the sit pose, which is unknowable here, so its default could not be *provably*
equal to today's numbers.

**Do not read the eye bone.** Two reasons, and the second is the decisive one: (i) bone transforms
resolve during the animation update, so an `OnUpdate` read can be a frame stale; (ii) **a camera
welded to a breathing, blinking head bone makes the board swim.** `SeatEyeBack`/`SeatEyeHeight` are
static constants — stable, no ordering hazard. If true head-tracking is ever wanted, the path is
`TryGetBoneTransformAnimation` in a late update.

### What `SeatOrbitRadius` / `SeatPitch` touch — and why the blend beats re-tuning them

Full consumer list, grepped:

- `BuildSeatAnchor` — ours.
- `RingRadius`'s `seatFootprint = SeatOrbitRadius·cos(SeatPitch)` = 32.1, clamping the ring inside
  the room.
- `ScreenFractionRect()` / `UiRectStyle()` — **dead code, no callers outside their own
  declaration.** CLAUDE.md still credits `ChessRing` with owning "the screen-rect UI math"; that
  line needs a correction. Load-bearing *good* news: it removes the only coupling that would have
  made seat-camera changes disturb engaged UI.

Because `SeatEyeBlend` leaves `SeatOrbitRadius`/`SeatPitch` untouched, the walk-up spot, the ring
clamp and the (dead) UI rect are unaffected at every blend value. **That is the point of the knob.**

### What actually breaks as the blend descends — and how to find the floor

**Picking does not break.** `SquareUnderCursor` is exact ray-plane math at any angle, and even the
far rank stays clickable. **Readability breaks:**

| | rank 1 (near) | rank 8 (far) | near/far |
|---|---|---|---|
| **default (blend 1)** | 5.3° / 123 px | 2.8° / 66 px | **1.9×** |
| **eye level (blend 0)** | 8.3° / 191 px | 1.6° / 37 px | **5.1×** |

At 5.1× foreshortening a knight on rank 6 occludes rank 8 outright — the same story the occlusion
table tells from the other side.

**How to find the floor in-editor:** hotload `SeatEyeBlend` down in steps (1.0 → 0.8 → 0.6 → 0.4)
and at each step ask three questions **in this order**: (1) can I read a piece on rank 8; (2) does
my own chest enter the bottom of frame; (3) does anything clip the near plane. The first "no" is the
floor. **Ship the last "yes" minus one step.** *(Near-clip is not a concern at the default: the
nearest body part is the head at 31.3 units and `ZNear` is 1.)*

The chair's outermost edge is x ≈ −43.8, beyond `seatFootprint` = 32.1 — but `RingRadius` reserves
`seatFootprint + 30` = 62.1, leaving **18.3 of slack**, so no wall collision. Chairs are ±9.75 in Y
against the table's ±33, so **if tables don't collide, chairs can't**.

### Helmets, masks and cosmetics

**The engine already did the hard part:** `ClothingContainer.Dressing.cs:228` does
`go.Tags.Add("clothing")` on every spawned clothing GameObject. So the whole requirement is:

```csharp
// our avatar only, while seated
foreach ( var go in clothingGosUnderBody ) go.Tags.Set( "viewer", true );
cam.RenderExcludeTags.Add( "viewer" );   // idempotent; Contains-guarded
```

`"viewer"` is the engine's own first-person tag (`PlayerController.Camera.cs:56-58` adds it to
`RenderExcludeTags`). **Add it explicitly anyway** — while seated the controller is disabled, so
`ModifyCamera` is not running to re-add it.

**Do not `RenderExcludeTags.Add("clothing")`** — that strips the *opponent's* clothes too. Tag our
GameObjects, not the class. Remote viewers must keep seeing our swag.

**The tag must go on each cosmetic GO individually** — the check is
`if (RenderExcludeTags.HasAny(c.GameObject.Tags)) continue;` (`CameraComponent.cs:512`), per
GameObject. **Do not assume inheritance to children.** That the engine tags each clothing GO
separately rather than relying on `Body` is the corroboration.

> **The open question is answered: `Component.Enabled` is NOT networked.** There is no `[Sync]` on
> it and no enabled-sync in `Scene/Networking/`. So `HideLocalAvatar`'s assertion is **correct** and
> seated players are **not** currently invisible to others. The tag mechanism is still the
> recommendation — "not networked" and "provably per-camera" are different guarantees, and only the
> second is what this needs.

> ### ⚠️ The snapshot trap — this one will bite
>
> `GameObject.Serialize.cs:211` serialises `Tags`, and `ModelRenderer.BodyGroups` is a plain
> serialised `[Property]`. CLAUDE.md's #12 rule: **a joining client rebuilds `NetworkMode.Snapshot`
> objects from the host's LIVE state**, and `Body` is `NetworkMode: 2` (Snapshot).
>
> So if the **host** is seated with `Head` off or a `"viewer"` tag on a networked GO, **a joiner
> rebuilds the host's avatar headless — and nothing ever corrects it**, because `LobbyPlayer.OnUpdate`
> early-returns on `IsProxy`. Same mechanism as the music board, different victim.
>
> Two mitigations, both required:
> 1. **Clothing GOs are created locally** by `ApplyAsync` on each client and are in nobody's
>    snapshot → tagging them is safe. Prefer them.
> 2. For anything on the networked `Body` (the `Head` bodygroup), **add a proxy-side per-frame
>    restore *before* the `IsProxy` early-return**: restore the full bodygroup mask and clear
>    `"viewer"`. This mirrors the engine's own `UpdateBodyVisibility()`, which runs for proxies
>    precisely to force `viewer = false` (`PlayerController.Animation.cs:117-127`). Cite that
>    precedent in the comment.

## The hands

**IK needs no GameObjects.** `SkinnedModelRenderer.SetIk(name, Transform)` takes a Transform
directly (`Parameters.cs:163-171`); `CitizenAnimationHelper`'s `IkLeftHand`/`IkRightHand`
**GameObject** properties are editor sugar over it. Lerp a `Transform` in code and pass it. The
animgraph carries `ik.hand_left.{enabled,position,rotation}` and `ik.hand_right.{…}` — the IK is
real.

> ⚠️ `SetIk` does `tx = WorldTransform.ToLocal(tx)` — **it wants a WORLD transform** and converts
> internally.

**Targets** (station-local → world via `Station.WorldTransform.PointToWorld`):

- **Float above a hovered movable piece:** `ring.SquareLocalPosition(file, rank) + Vector3.Up *
  HandHoverHeight` (≈ 6 — above a 4.8 pawn, below a 9.6 king). **Never touching it.**
- **Idle, elbows on table:** hands at station-local `(side * 26, ±10, 31)`. The board frame's
  half-extent is 21.75 and the tabletop's is 30, so **x = ±26 lands in the 8.25-wide X margin** —
  clear of the board, on the table.
- **Right hand works, left hand idles.** Chess is played one-handed.

> **This spends a margin CLAUDE.md says is spent.** The file states *"±X are kept clear — they are
> the seat cameras' sightlines"*. M13 puts **hands** there. That is not a violation — the hands are
> the sightline's subject — but the margin-budget section **must** be updated to say the ±X margins
> now carry each seat's hands and forearms, or the next reader files it as a bug. A documentation
> obligation, not a tidy-up.

### Fingers — the find

`CitizenAnimationHelper` exposes no finger control. **The animgraph does.**
`citizen.vanmgrph` declares `holdtype_pose_hand` — a `CFloatAnimParameter`, default 0, min 0,
max 1 — and its own comment node says, verbatim:

> *"Used in the \"HoldItem\" holdtype. References a 100-frame (\*) animation, so that every digit
> from 0.00 to 1.00 can land cleanly on the full pose, rather than in-between. Going halfway
> between specific poses e.g. 0.125 is perfectly valid, though! The relevant parameter is
> holdtype_pose_hand (float). (\* Currently only has 18 poses as of the time of writing this
> comment node.) ALSO DEFINES THE POS/ROT OF THE HOLD_\* BONES."*

Its consumer is a `CCycleControlAnimNode` whose note reads *"Child2 from the **finger blending**
nodes needs to not be reset."*

**So `holdtype_pose_hand` is a continuous, lerpable finger-blend.** That is exactly the "move the
fingers closer, as if thinking about picking it up" lever — no crude `HoldItem`/`None` toggle.

```csharp
r.Set( "holdtype", 4 );                  // HoldTypes.HoldItem
r.Set( "holdtype_handedness", 1 );       // Hand.Right
r.Set( "holdtype_pose_hand", t );        // 0..1, lerped
```

**Verified:** the param, its type, its range, its finger role. **Not verified:** which end is open
vs closed, and whether it reads well with nothing in hand. (`holdtype_pose`, a float −1..16, is a
second unexposed knob if needed.)

### Pickup, drop, and abandoning

**Do not sync pickup/drop.** The move is already replicated on the seam, so every client sees it at
the same instant and drives the same animation from `LastMoveUci`. **Sync only what has no other
evidence** — hover and selection.

Drive it exactly as `TableSounds` does: on a `BoardDiff.Move` by the seat being animated, hand →
from-square, close fingers, lift, travel, open at to-square. `BoardDiff.Between` gives the
classification for free and already covers a lichess game.

**The abandon rule:** every frame, if the game has advanced past the move being animated, drop the
animation and re-target. **The game is authority; the animation is decoration.**

## The sync

**Only hover + selection crosses the wire.** Everything else — the pose, the pickup, the tuck, the
tint — is derived locally from already-replicated state.

**Home: `LobbyPlayer`, owner-synced.** Not `ChessStation`, which is `[Sync(FromHost)]` — hover is
the *client's* own truth, not the host's. `LobbyPlayer.GambitName` is the precedent: a bare `[Sync]`
on a networked, player-owned component, published by the owner behind an explicit change gate.

```csharp
/// Packed hover+selection, published by the OWNER. -1 = none.
/// One int, so the change gate is one comparison and a mouse move
/// within a square costs nothing.
[Sync] public int HandState { get; set; } = -1;

static int Pack( int hover, int selected ) => (hover + 1) | ((selected + 1) << 7);
```

7 bits each (0..64 after the +1 bias). Assigned only on change — the same instinct as
`PaintHighlights`' hash gate, but better: the hash gate skips *work*, this skips a *network write*.

**Bandwidth:** hover changes only when the cursor crosses a square boundary — a handful of int
writes per second per seated player, and **zero** while the cursor sits still or nobody is seated.
`[Sync]` diffs anyway; the gate is belt-and-braces and matches the house style.

**`_hoverSquare` already means the right thing.** It is assigned only *after* `UpdateInput`'s gate
(`!source.IsMyTurn && !premoving`, `PendingPromotion != null`, `CameraSettled`). So "hovered"
already means *"a square this player can act on"* — exactly the milestone's "if a movable piece is
hovered". No new predicate.

**Why this survives the lichess seam.** The payload is a raw square index plus a selection — **no
game-source knowledge crosses the wire**. An observer needs only "which square to float the hand
over". `LichessGameController` being un-networked is irrelevant: the hovering player derives their
own hover from their own source and publishes a square. Which is also why a third kind of game gets
this for free.

**Resolving a seat's avatar:** the local player if `ChessStation.Active == Station && ActiveSeat ==
seat` (covers the optimistic-claim window before the host's `[Sync]` lands); otherwise scan
`Scene.GetAllComponents<LobbyPlayer>()` for `Network.Owner?.SteamId == station.SeatSteamId(seat)`.
Cache on change, not per frame.

## The driver

`World/SeatedTerry.cs` — a `Component`, **one per station**, added by `ChessRing.BuildInternal()`
beside `TableSounds` and wired identically. **`TableSounds` is the template, and the shape is the
point:**

- `IBoardGame Source => BoardGame.Source( Controller, Lichess );` — **the identical expression**, so
  what you see, what you hear and what the hands do are the same game. A third kind of game gets
  seated hands **by existing**.
- `bool Mine => ChessStation.Active == Station;`
- **Source swap → `Baseline(src)` and return. ADOPT, never zero** — adopting means a swap onto an
  already-over game does not re-trigger a pickup. Abandon any in-flight animation on the swap: the
  FEN jump across two unrelated games is exactly the invented transition `TableSounds` warns about.
- **Silent for tables nobody plays:** early-out on `!Station.AnySeatTaken`.
- Runs on every client for every station, **purely locally**: reads replicated state, writes
  animation params. A missed frame costs one frame of hand position.

> This is CLAUDE.md's rule being **honoured**, not imitated: *"If a new reactive feature has you
> typing `LocalGameController`, that is the same mistake starting again."* Sound made exactly that
> mistake and lichess games were silent for two milestones with nothing wrong in any diff.

### The pure extraction

**`Code/Chess/TerryPose.cs`** — Sandbox-free, plain data in, plain data out, following
`CapturedMaterial` (plain `char[64]`) and `BoardDiff` (plain FEN + ply):

```csharp
public enum HandPhase { Idle, Hover, Selected, Lifting, Carrying, Dropping }
public readonly record struct HandPose( HandPhase Phase, int Square, float Height, float FingerClose, float Weight );
public static HandPose Resolve( int hover, int selected, string lastMoveUci, bool isMyTurn,
                                int ply, float sinceMove, in HandPose prev );
```

**No `Vector3`** — that is `Sandbox`. Return a square index plus scalars and let the driver map them
through `SquareLocalPosition`. That constraint is what keeps it runnable here.

Worth extracting because the state machine has edges that read as obviously correct and aren't: a
turn flipping mid-lift, a selection cleared under a carry, a move landing while hovering elsewhere,
a `PendingPromotion` parked. **The "abandon on a new turn" rule is exactly what a dotnet harness
proves and review doesn't** — drive real games through `ChessGame` and assert no phase survives a
ply change. Same method that caught the capture-promotion bug in `CapturedMaterial`.

## Seat switch: stand → walk → sit

Replaces the SCHWOOP (`b4cd9cc`).

### Path and duration

Chairs sit at station-local (∓36, 0). The table blocks a straight line (collider x ±31.5, y ±34.5;
corner radius 46.7). Use a **polar sweep with a lerped radius**: θ 0° → 180°, r 36 →
`SwitchArcRadius` (52) → 36. Constant r = 36 puts the midpoint at (0, ±36) — only **1.5 outboard of
the collider**, so the shoulders clip. r = 52 clears the corner (46.7) by 5.3 ✔.

Path length ≈ π × mean radius ≈ **141 units**.

**`SeatSwitchTime = 0.6f` is wrong: 141/0.6 = 235 u/s** — between walk (110) and run (320), an
unmotivated jog. **Propose `SeatSwitchTime = 1.3f`** → 108 u/s ≈ the 110 walk speed.

**Also change the easing.** Ease-out cubic starts at maximum speed — with the legs now driven from
real velocity, that is a foot-slide on frame 1. Use **ease-in-out (smoothstep)**: a walk accelerates
and decelerates.

### What drives the walk

**A scripted lerp, not the PlayerController.** No navmesh exists in the repo, re-enabling the
controller would hand back mouselook mid-transition, and the lerp is the same lever `BeginEngage`
already pulls — in reverse:

```csharp
// BeginEngage STOMPS these to 0 because the controller is off and nothing else writes them.
// The switch DRIVES them from the lerp velocity, so the legs actually move.
var vel = (pos - _lastSwitchPos) / Time.Delta;
_bodyRenderer.Set( "move_groundspeed", vel.WithZ(0).Length );
_bodyRenderer.Set( "move_x", WorldRotation.Forward.Dot( vel ) );
_bodyRenderer.Set( "move_y", WorldRotation.Right.Dot( vel ) );
_bodyRenderer.Set( "move_z", vel.z );
```

Plus `r.Set("sit", 0)` for the duration, back to 1 on arrival. Yaw along the path tangent so the
terry walks forwards, then `LookAt(toBoard)` on arrival.

### The camera does not ride the body

Today it rides as a child of a *rotating* avatar and the arc reads as a head turn. Riding a
**141-unit walk** would be nauseating.

**Reuse `UpdateLockedCamera` unchanged:** set `_camFromPos/_camFromRot` from the current camera,
`_engageTime = 0`, blend to the new anchor over `SeatSwitchTime`. The camera is a child but its
**world** transform is stomped every frame, so parenting is already irrelevant. Both endpoints are
absolute (camera → new anchor, body → new chair), so they **cannot drift out of sync** — the exact
property the SCHWOOP comment was protecting, preserved with no new camera math.

Both anchors sit at z = 80.12 and the tabletop tops out at 30, so a straight lerp passes **50 units
above the table** — no clipping, and it reads as your viewpoint gliding over the board. One change
needed: `CamBlendTime` becomes a variable (0.35 engage, `SeatSwitchTime` switch).

### The rest

- **`InstantReseat` stays** as the anchors-not-built fallback (point it at `SeatSitWorldPosition`).
- **Callers unchanged** — the lichess shareable-link colour chip still calls `LobbyPlayer.SwitchSeat`;
  `ChessStation.SwitchActiveSeat` still does `RequestLeave(old)` + `RequestEnter(new)`; the
  taken-seat and live-game refusals stay.
- **Replication:** the avatar transform is owner-networked, so a **lerped walk replicates as
  movement** (a teleport replicates as a snap). The walk animation on remote clients is **free** — a
  proxy's `PlayerController` is enabled and derives the cycle from observed velocity, the same
  mechanism `BeginEngage`'s comment already relies on. We stomp params only on the **owner**, where
  the controller is off.

## Risks, ranked

> **The two hardest problems in M13 are networking, and neither was in the brief.** The brief treats
> arms-only rendering as the hard part; it is impossible *and* unnecessary. Risks 1 and 2 are the
> milestone's real difficulty, and both are invisible from a single-player editor session — you need
> a second instance to see either.

1. **A proxy's `PlayerController` is enabled and IS driving the animator**, so it may fight the
   seated pose on remote clients — `PlayerController.cs:156` writes `Renderer.WorldRotation` from
   `EyeAngles`. `LobbyPlayer.OnUpdate` early-returns on `IsProxy` and never disables it. *This is the
   direct consequence of the retracted finding: because `Renderer` self-heals, proxies animate.*
   The fix, if needed, is to disable a proxy's controller while its seat is occupied — **but check
   first**; Terries look fine standing at tables today, so it may already be benign.
   **Mechanism verified; outcome unknown. The single highest-value thing to check in the editor.**
2. **The bodygroup/tag snapshot leak** — `BodyGroups` and `Tags` ride the host's live state to
   joiners, and the `IsProxy` early-return means nothing corrects it. Documented precedent (#12, the
   music board); mitigation designed above. *Mechanism verified against CLAUDE.md's own rule.*
3. **The sit pose is unknowable here, and five numbers depend on it.** `SeatEyeHeight` (49),
   `SeatSitBack` (36), the belly 0.5, the head 0.37, the shoulder 0.81 all rest on *"seated eye ≈ 31
   above the pad, torso half-depth ≈ 5.5"* — **estimates from human proportion scaled to a 72-unit
   citizen, not measurements.** If the pose differs, the frame analysis shifts. *Mitigation: all are
   `[Property]` floats; checklist step 5 calibrates them first.* **The biggest unknown by far.**
4. **The belly clearance is 0.5** — the tightest number in M13, resting on an estimated torso
   half-depth of 5.5 that could easily be ±2. `SeatSitBack` must stay a knob.
5. **`SeatEyeBlend`'s floor is higher than hoped** — 5.1× foreshortening at blend 0, and the torso
   intrudes with no clean removal. First-person may simply not be playable. *Arithmetic verified.*
6. **Everything is an edit-and-hotload loop, not a scene-tweak loop.** `ChessRing` is **not in
   `lobby.scene`** — `LobbyRoom` self-provisions it, so every M13 constant is a code default, exactly
   as CLAUDE.md warns for `SpectatorWall`. Not a bug; a real ergonomic cost across ~16 tunables.
   *Mitigation: `gambit_terry`.*
7. **`holdtype_pose_hand` polarity and legibility** — the param, range and finger role are verified;
   **which end is closed, and whether it reads with nothing in hand, are not.** *Fallback:
   `holdtype_pose` (−1..16).*
8. **Hand IK may not reach rank 8** from a seated shoulder — the arm snaps or over-extends. Not

9. **The stand-walk-sit path clips the table** if the radius isn't lerped (a naive r = 36 arc passes

10. **Tube rotation mapping** — Z-lathe vs `LookAt`'s +X. Cannot be run here.
